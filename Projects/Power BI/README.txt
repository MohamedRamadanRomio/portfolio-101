Diabetes dashboard project 

First chart (top left): the clustered column chart shows the relation between age groups and the incidence of T2DM and despite old age (>50 yo) is considered a risk factor but the increase in the percentage of patients from young adult (18-30) affected with T2DM shows how lifestyle and diet change over decades affected the incidence of chronic diseases and it's clearly associated with increased obesity at young age.

Second chart: the clustered column chart shows clearly the relation between weight and T2DM as obesity is a huge risk factor and the mostly affected BMI category.

Third chart: it's a pie chart used to show the relation between number of pregnancies and T2DM as pregnancy is a diabetogenic condition and may cause GDM which may later progress to T2DM and it shows clearly high risk group (>=6 pregnancies) are the one affected the most and nullipara (0 pregnancies) are the least affected 

Fourth chart: clustered bar chart shows the average of glucose for diabetic and non-diabetic patient 

Fifth chart: clustered bar chart shows the average of insulin for diabetic and non-diabetic patient and it shows a clear sign of T2DM (specially early stage) which is increased Insulin as there is insulin resistance (Hyperinsulinemia occurs)

And finally in this dataset 228 out of 691 patients were diagnosed with diabetes which gives prevalence of 33% which is higher than the global average (this data is influenced by specific geography and population (Pima Indiana women in Arizona))
